Habits

Routines or behaviors performed regularly, often *automatically

*Sometimes for good, sometimes for evil (or at least un-good)

Stage 1: Cue

Make it Obvious

Stage 1: Cue - Make it Obvious

Create Awareness of Existing Habits

  • Create a Habit Scorecard with a list your daily habits rated as effective (+), ineffective (-), or neutral (=)
  • Literally call them out as you perform them
  • Don't make any changes or judgements yet; just observe
Stage 1: Cue - Make it Obvious

Create Obvious Cues

  • Create an Implementation Intention to eliminate ambiguity: "I will [BEHAVIOR] at [TIME] in [LOCATION]"
  • Leveraging existing cues with Habit Stacking: "After [CURRENT HABIT], I will [NEW HABIT]"
A Framework for Changing Behavior

Stage 2: Craving

Make it Attractive

Stage 2: Craving - Make it Attractive

Leverage Temptation Bundling

  • Increase likelihood of performing less enjoyable behaviors by pairing with more enjoyable behaviors
  • Link an action you WANT to do with something you NEED
  • "After [HABIT I NEED], I will [HABIT I WANT]"
Stage 2: Craving - Make it Attractive

Leverage Cultural Influences & Social Norms

  • Engage with others who share your desired behavior and interests
  • Understand that changing habits to fit with our social groups (the close, the many, and the powerful) is attractive and requires less effort

Stage 3: Response

Make it Easy

Stage 3: Response - Make it Easy

Design Your Environment to Minimize Friction

  • Choose locations that fit well with your daily routine
  • Prepare locations / resources in advance
Stage 3: Response - Make it Easy

MOAR Strategies!!!

  • Make habits ridiculously small (to leverage momentum)
  • Leverage technology and one-time decisions to automate habits

Stage 4: Reward

Make it Satisfying

Stage 4: Reward - Make it Satisfying

Reward Yourself Immediately

  • Immediate rewards > Delayed rewards
  • Use rewards inline with your goals immediately following habit
  • If doing nothing is the habit, find ways to make even that rewarding
Stage 4: Reward - Make it Satisfying

Leverage Habit Tracking

  • Provides visual cues to remind you to take action
  • Provides motivation to keep you going
  • Provides satisfying proof you are making progress towards goals and desired identity
A Framework for Changing Behavior

Invert the stages to break bad habits

  1. Cue - Make it Invisible
  2. Craving - Make it Unattractive
  3. Response - Make it Difficult
  4. Reward - Make it Unsatisfying

Call to Action

Commit to using what you've learned about habits & the framework for behavior change to make one small change.

WHEN it works, keep going! Remember to keep the habits aligned with WHO you are.
